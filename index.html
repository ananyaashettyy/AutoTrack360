About Us

The ‘AutoTrack360’ is a digital platform developed to streamline the process of reporting waste-related issues directly to the Mangaluru City Corporation (MCC).

  • Report garbage or waste collection issues in their neighborhood.
  • View the status of their complaints and verify if action has been taken.
  • Explore tips and ideas on recycling and managing waste at home.

Traditionally, raising complaints about daily waste problems to the municipality has been a time-consuming process. This system aims to make it easier and more transparent. With a mobile phone or any internet-enabled device, users can quickly lodge their concerns. The platform automatically forwards the complaints to the respective MCC ward officials, allowing them to acknowledge and act upon the reports. Citizens are also notified once their issues are resolved, ensuring clear communication and accountability.

Guidelines implementation must for proper management of medical waste

Proper implementation of medical waste guidelines is essential in Mangaluru to protect public health. The city generates around 1.5 to 1.8 tonnes of biomedical waste daily. Major hospitals like Wenlock, KMC, AJ Hospital, and Father Muller are required to follow the Bio-Medical Waste Management Rules, 2016. However, studies show that not all facilities follow proper disposal methods, with some mixing waste into municipal bins. Strict enforcement and staff training are crucial for safe waste handling and environmental safety.

  • 01 “Problems in Mangaluru"

    Most hospitals in Mangaluru use:Autoclaving (steam sterilization),Incineration for high-risk waste ,Shredding & disinfection for plastics and packaging. Some facilities use outsourced services like Common Biomedical Waste Treatment Facilities (CBWTFs) located in or near Mangaluru for final disposal.

  • 02 Implementation Methods

    Hospitals in Mangaluru use color-coded bins and barcoded bags for biomedical waste, which is collected and treated at a Common Biomedical Waste Treatment Facility (CBWTF). The Karnataka State Pollution Control Board (KSPCB) monitors these processes and mandates regular reporting.

  • 03 DChallenges Faced

    Despite systems in place, small clinics often don’t follow proper disposal rules. Delays in waste collection and lack of inspections lead to overflowing bins. Many waste workers still handle medical waste without gloves or PPE, increasing health risks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Waste management regulations and disposal methods.

  • What is the best way to dispose of household waste in Mangaluru?

    In Mangaluru, household waste should be segregated into wet (organic) and dry (non-biodegradable) waste before disposal. Wet waste should be composted or handed to municipal collectors, while dry waste should be cleaned and given to recyclers or designated bins.

  • Can I report illegal dumping or garbage issues in Mangaluru?

    Yes, citizens can report uncollected garbage or illegal dumping to the Mangaluru City Corporation (MCC) through helpline numbers or apps like OneTouch Mangaluru, which allows for easy complaint logging and tracking.

  • What are some simple ways to manage waste at home?

    Start by segregating waste into wet and dry categories. Compost kitchen waste, reuse plastic containers, and avoid single-use plastics. Participate in local recycling drives or hand over recyclable materials to authorized collectors.

  • What are the different types of waste?

    1. Wet waste- Wet waste consists of kitchen waste - including vegetable and fruit peels and pieces, tea leaves, coffee grounds, eggshells, bones and entrails, fish scales, as well as cooked food (both veg and non-veg).

    2. Dry Waste- Paper, plastics, metal, glass, rubber, thermocol, styrofoam, fabric, leather, rexine, wood – anything that can be kept for an extended period without decomposing is classified as dry waste.

    3 .Hazardous waste- Household hazardous waste or HHW include three sub-categories – E-waste; toxic substances such as paints, cleaning agents, solvents, insecticides and their containers, other chemicals; and biomedical waste.

    4. E-waste- E-waste or electronic waste consists of batteries, computer parts, wires, electrical equipment of any kind, electrical and electronic toys, remotes, watches, cell phones, bulbs, tube lights and CFLs.

    5. Biomedical waste- This includes used menstrual cloth, sanitary napkins, disposable diapers, bandages and any material that is contaminated with blood or other body fluids.

  • What are ways of storing the waste at homes?

    1. Dry waste- Store it in a bag in the utility area after cleaning and drying till it is picked up. No food residue must be left in the bottles and packets. Clean them as you would to reuse them. If clothes are totally unusable, or very damaged, they are categorized as dry waste. If clothes are soiled with body fluids, they become sanitary waste. If they are soiled with paint, or any chemicals, they are HHW (household hazardous waste).

    2. E-waste- Store them in separate container which is kept closed, away from moisture and in which nothing else is put.

  • How do I dispose my waste?

    1. Compost your wet waste at home- Home composting can easily be done in any aerated container. Get more details on composting and begin composting today!

    2 Compost your wet waste at the community level- If you live in a large apartment building, a community composting system like tank composting could be set up for all the wet waste from the residents. If not, the wet waste can be given out every day to your Municipality collection system.

    3 Biomedical waste has to be wrapped separately in a newspaper and handed over to the municipality waste collection system. Expired medicines and injections, used syringes also have to be disposed in the same manner. Paint and other hazardous waste like cosmetics, mosquito repellents, tube lights etc have to be stored separately and handed over to the Municipal collection system.
